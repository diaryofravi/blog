Tonight, I am on a bus from Bangalore to Goa, with 1.5 kg of Kaju and Horlicks burfi from Kanti Sweets on my lap, and tears I am trying very hard to hold back.

Tomorrow, I take delivery of my first motorcycle — bought with my own money, on my 34th birthday.

### The Quote That Found Me

There is a scene in *The Shawshank Redemption* where Red finally boards the bus and says:

> *"I find I'm so excited, I can barely sit still or hold a thought in my head. I think it's the excitement only a free man can feel — a free man at the start of a long journey whose conclusion is uncertain."*

I was a kid when I first watched that film. I did not know then that one day, sitting on a night bus to Goa, I would feel exactly what he felt.

### The Narva Ferry

It started on a ferry.

I was very young when I saw a Yamaha RD350 on the Narva ferry boat. It looked different — a little bulky compared to the RX100s and Kaka's KB100 that I had grown up seeing in the village. But something about it stayed with me. The shape, the presence, the way it just *sat* there on the deck like it owned the river.

The passion grew quietly after that. The way things do when you cannot do anything about them yet.

### The Years In Between

Papa was strict. He never let me take my bicycle to Porvorim. I was born premature, and maybe that was the reason he always worried — though I did not fully understand it then.

I started saving money once, with the genuine hope of buying a motorcycle. But the money was taken. More than once. Everyone knows who.

I went to college on the back of Rohit's bike. I never had a phone to listen to music on the way to class. I never rode into campus on my own motorcycle the way I had imagined as a kid. Some dreams just sit quietly in a corner and wait.

When Papa once asked me — genuinely asked — whether I liked Rohit's R15 or Hari's Bullet, I did not have the wisdom to answer honestly. Spending time with Rammu had pulled me toward the P220 for a while, but if I am truthful, I always wanted a Honda or a Yamaha. I was just forcing myself to want something more within reach.

I finished my diploma and degree, but the dream of riding to college on my own motorcycle never came true. I never rode my bicycle to school, never had a phone to listen to music on the way to class. Being an introvert made all of this harder than it sounds.

### The Duke That Almost Was

In 2012, KTM launched the Duke 200. Then the 390 in 2013.

I wanted one immediately. The naked stance, the aggression, the value — it felt like what the RD350 must have felt like in the 1980s. Revolutionary and just right. I told myself: *this is the one.*

But life had other timing.

### The Triumph That Chose Me

Kaka gave me a 1991 Bullet once. It had issues, but it was mine — for six long years, I held on to it.

Then the universe asked me to let it go.

And in its place, somehow — I still do not fully understand how I pulled this off — came a **Triumph Speed 400, Carnival Red**.

I am older now, not yet settled in life, and not flush with money. But my Gods were working. Mahadev, Ganesha, Shani Dev — and Aie, always Aie — along with some patient, stubborn corner of the universe that kept this dream alive longer than I thought possible.

People will say it is just a motorcycle.

They are not wrong. But they are also not entirely right.

### Pitru Paksh

I know this is Pitru Paksh — the fortnight when we are not supposed to buy new things, not until October 14th.

But I believe my *pitrus* blessed this. I believe they are the reason it happened at all.

Tomorrow is the day.

I am on a bus, eating burfi, trying to stay calm, turning 34 at midnight somewhere on the highway between Bangalore and Goa.

*A free man at the start of a long journey.*
