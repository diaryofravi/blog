Food habits

Clean, consistent, and cooked at home.

I haven't consumed sugar for over 18 months. No aerated drinks, no processed snacks. I cook almost everything myself, which keeps me in control of what goes in.

Breakfast
🥚
Eggs
Boiled or scrambled. High protein, keeps me full until lunch without being heavy.
Lunch
🍗
Chicken steak & rice
Pan-seared chicken, plain rice. Protein and carbs — no overthinking.
Evening snack
🌱
Black chana or sweet potato
Complex carbs and fibre — keeps energy steady without a spike.
Dinner
🍽️
Chicken steak & rice
Same as lunch. Consistency removes decision fatigue.
☕

Coffee

Not just a drink — a small, deliberate ritual.

My first coffee of the day is always after breakfast, never before. I have it alone — no phone, no conversation, no background noise if I can help it. Just the cup and a few quiet minutes before the day really starts.

It's a small ritual, but it matters. That window between breakfast and sitting down to study is mine. The coffee is just the anchor for it.

I keep it black or with minimal milk. No sugar — that's been gone for a long time. The taste is enough.
